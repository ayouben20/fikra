Discover Our Fabrics

Cotton

Best For: Everyday apparel, quilting, home decor (curtains, pillows).
Pros: Breathable, soft, easy to care for, versatile.
Cons: Prone to wrinkling, can shrink.

Linen

Best For: Summer clothing (trousers, shirts, dresses), tablecloths, napkins.
Pros: Highly breathable, strong, absorbent, gets softer with washes.
Cons: Wrinkles very easily, can be expensive.

Silk

Best For: Luxury apparel (gowns, blouses), scarves, lingerie, ties.
Pros: Luxurious feel, beautiful drape, strong natural fiber.
Cons: Expensive, requires delicate care, water spots easily.

Wool

Best For: Outerwear (coats, jackets), suits, sweaters, blankets.
Pros: Very warm, durable, water-resistant, wrinkle-resistant.
Cons: Can be itchy, requires special care (dry clean or hand wash).

Polyester

Best For: Activewear, blended apparel, upholstery, fleece.
Pros: Durable, wrinkle-resistant, quick-drying, retains color well.
Cons: Not breathable, can feel synthetic, melts at high heat.

Denim

Best For: Jeans, jackets, overalls, bags, sturdy workwear.
Pros: Extremely durable, versatile look, fades uniquely over time.
Cons: Can be stiff initially, heavy, takes long to dry.
